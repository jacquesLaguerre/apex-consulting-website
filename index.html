Frequently Asked Questions

Trusted guidance to help you make confident financial decisions.

1. What documents should I have to file my taxes?
You should have a valid ID, Social Security cards (for you and dependents), W-2s, 1099s, and any documents showing income, deductions, or credits. If you’re unsure, have everything you received and we’ll review it with you.
2. How long does it take to get my refund?
If you claimed the Child Tax Credit or Earned Income Credit, the IRS cannot release your refund until at least February 15, and it may arrive around February 27 or later. For most other refunds, the IRS states that about 90% are issued within 21 days. Use “Track Your Refund” to check your status.
3. What if I didn’t file my taxes last year?
We can help you file prior-year returns. It’s important to get caught up as soon as possible to avoid additional penalties or delays with refunds.
4. Do you offer electronic filing (e-file)?
Yes. We electronically file most returns for faster processing and quicker refunds compared to mailing paper returns.
